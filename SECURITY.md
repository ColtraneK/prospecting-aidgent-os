# Security — the trust posture

This is the part the whole thing rests on. Read it before connecting anything, and say it out loud in any live demo.

## The commitments

1. **Read-first, act-never-without-consent.** The agent reads and drafts. It does not send, post, connect, comment, or delete without your explicit approval. Every outward action is something you do yourself.
2. **Your data stays yours.** Everything runs in your own accounts, for your own decisions. Read-only wherever possible. You control the connections and can revoke them anytime.
3. **Reversible by design.** Nothing fires blind. The agent proposes, you approve.
4. **Respect explicit privacy requests.** If a person asks for their data handled a certain way, that governs.

## How it shows up here

- Sourcing is assisted, human-reviewed research, not bulk scraping. You review every row before anything happens.
- The default method uses the public web and needs no sign-in.
- The LinkedIn method (Codex + Playwright) drives your own logged-in session over CDP: no stored credentials, no automated login, read-only, paced politely.
- The sheet never sends anything. You tick the boxes and reach out yourself.

## Respect platform terms

Automated scraping and bot browsing violate many sites' terms. Keep volumes low, keep it human-paced, use your own session, and review everything. If you would not do it by hand, do not script it.
